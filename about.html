ME & MY LIFE

Hi! I’m Breeze, a student at USC in Los Angeles, majoring in Design, Technology, and Business, with a minor and planned masters in Computer Science. I was born and raised in Hawai’i, where the unique culture instilled in me a deep appreciation for community and the sacredness of nature.

Growing up on a 30 mile wide island surrounded by ocean, water sports were always a part of my day. Now, I’ve become a successful collegiate athlete in sailing, with a love for surfing, kiting, and windsurfing. These disciplines fueled my adventurous spirit and taught me invaluable lessons in dedication, work ethic, and teamwork. The ocean isn’t just a part of my life, its a part of my heart.

Volunteer work is a cornerstone of my life. I frequently engage in hands-on environmental protection initiatives and work with programs that empower young women in STEM. For the past eight years, I’ve been dedicated to saving turtles with Malama i na Honu. I’ve also championed environmental legislation, such as bill 40, which bans many single-use plastics in Hawai’i. In addition, through my work with Amnesty International, I raised funds that provided 5 people with a lifetime of clean water.

In my free-time, I immerse myself in my writing, and in exploring language or art. I’ve written a book focused on protecting honey bees, but mainly write just for my own enjoyment. I dabble in painting and illustration, as well as wood-working, 3D-printing, and welding.

Professionally, I study programming in the hopes of working in software development. I enjoy creating small games and programs, learning about algorithms, and designing apps/websites. Recently I’ve sparked an interest in learning about cyber-security, and hope to explore that more this fall & winter. Please check out my projects and my GitHub for more.
